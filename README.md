h1>Power BI Internship Tasks</h1>

<div class="task">
        <h2>Task 1: Visualize Overall Sales Performance</h2>
        <h3>Objective:</h3>
        <p>Visualize overall sales performance to monitor trends and make data-driven business decisions.</p>
        <h3>Key Areas Covered:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Total Sales: A KPI card summarizing the total sales revenue over a given period.</li>
            <li>Monthly or Quarterly Growth: Line or bar charts displaying sales growth trends over time.</li>
            <li>Top-Selling Products: A bar chart listing the best-selling products and their sales figures.</li>
            <li>Sales by Region: A map or table breaking down sales performance by region or country.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Line Charts: To show sales trends.</li>
            <li>Bar Charts: For product performance comparison.</li>
            <li>KPI Cards: To display key figures like total sales, average order value, etc.</li>
            <li>Filters: Interactive slicers to filter sales data by date, product category, or region.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Identify seasonal trends, highlight best-performing products, and recognize regions with potential growth.</p>
    </div>

<div class="task">
        <h2>Task 2: Analyze Customer Demographics and Purchasing Behavior</h2>
        <h3>Objective:</h3>
        <p>Analyze customer demographics and understand purchasing behavior.</p>
        <h3>Key Areas Covered:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Customer Segmentation: Pie charts showing the percentage of customers in different segments (age groups, income levels, etc.).</li>
            <li>Average Order Value: Calculation of the typical purchase amount, often displayed in a KPI card.</li>
            <li>Repeat Purchase Rate: A measure of customer loyalty, presented in a gauge or bar chart.</li>
            <li>Geographical Analysis: Maps highlighting where the most active customers are located.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Pie Charts: For demographic breakdown.</li>
            <li>Scatter Plots: Analyzing spending habits against demographics.</li>
            <li>Tables: Detailed view of customer data for in-depth analysis.</li>
            <li>Filters: Options to slice data by demographic characteristics, regions, or purchase history.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Insights into which customer segments are most valuable and recommendations for targeted marketing efforts.</p>
    </div>

<div class="task">
        <h2>Task 3: Manage Inventory Levels</h2>
        <h3>Objective:</h3>
        <p>Manage inventory levels and optimize stock management.</p>
        <h3>Key Areas Covered:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Current Stock Levels: A bar chart or heatmap indicating product inventory levels.</li>
            <li>Reorder Alerts: Indicators or cards that show items needing restocking.</li>
            <li>Inventory Turnover: Line graphs tracking how often stock is sold and replaced.</li>
            <li>Out-of-Stock Analysis: Tables listing items that are frequently out of stock.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Heatmaps: To visualize inventory density.</li>
            <li>Bar Graphs: Showing the number of items per category.</li>
            <li>Tables: Listing critical stock items and their reorder status.</li>
            <li>Filters: Filters for different warehouses, product types, or time periods.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Highlight trends that may lead to stockouts or excess inventory and suggest actions for inventory optimization.</p>
    </div>

<div class="task">
        <h2>Task 4: Assess Marketing Campaign Performance</h2>
        <h3>Objective:</h3>
        <p>Assess the impact and performance of marketing campaigns.</p>
        <h3>Key Areas Covered:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Campaign Reach: Total number of people reached, shown in a KPI card.</li>
            <li>Conversion Rates: Percentage of people who took a desired action, like making a purchase.</li>
            <li>Return on Investment (ROI): A financial metric calculated and presented as a percentage or monetary value.</li>
            <li>Channel Effectiveness: A bar or line chart comparing the effectiveness of different marketing channels (email, social media, etc.).</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Funnel Charts: Illustrating the conversion process from leads to sales.</li>
            <li>Line Charts: To compare campaign performance over time.</li>
            <li>KPI Cards: Displaying critical metrics like ROI.</li>
            <li>Filters: Options to filter data by campaign type, channel, or date range.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Identify the most effective marketing strategies and areas where campaigns underperformed.</p>
    </div>

<div class="task">
        <h2>Task 5: Monitor Employee Performance</h2>
        <h3>Objective:</h3>
        <p>Monitor employee performance and productivity metrics.</p>
        <h3>Key Areas Covered:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Tasks Completed: Number of tasks or projects completed, presented in a bar or line chart.</li>
            <li>Hours Worked: Visualization of hours worked compared to productivity metrics.</li>
            <li>Performance Scores: Gauges or charts representing employee ratings or productivity levels.</li>
            <li>Department Comparison: A chart comparing performance across different teams or departments.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Bar Charts: For comparing productivity metrics between employees or departments.</li>
            <li>Gauges: Showing performance scores or productivity percentages.</li>
            <li>Tables: Detailed records of tasks and performance metrics.</li>
            <li>Filters: By department, employee role, or time period.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Identify high-performing teams, track productivity trends, and suggest improvements.</p>
    </div>

<div class="task">
        <h2>Task 6: Analyze Financial Data</h2>
        <h3>Objective:</h3>
        <p>Analyze financial data to understand budget utilization and financial health.</p>
        <h3>Key Areas Covered:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Revenue vs. Expenses: A waterfall or bar chart showing income and expenditure breakdowns.</li>
            <li>Budget Variance: Metrics indicating over- or under-spending in various categories.</li>
            <li>Net Profit: Calculation displayed in a KPI card, along with trends over time.</li>
            <li>Cash Flow Analysis: Line graphs depicting cash flow trends.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Waterfall Charts: To illustrate budget changes and variances.</li>
            <li>Tables: Listing revenue and expense details.</li>
            <li>KPI Cards: Highlighting net profit and budget utilization.</li>
            <li>Filters: By department, project, or financial year.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Provide a clear view of financial performance and budget management, with recommendations for financial adjustments.</p>
    </div>

<div class="task">

            <li>Risk Analysis: Tables listing project risks and their severity levels.</li>
            <li>Resource Allocation: Visualization of how resources are distributed across projects.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Visualizations:</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Gantt Charts: For project timelines.</li>
            <li>Progress Bars: Showing overall project completion.</li>
            <li>Tables: Detailed records of tasks and risk factors.</li>
            <li>Filters: By project phase, team, or risk level.</li>
        </ul>
        <h3>Analysis:</h3>
        <p>Identify project delays, highlight resource allocation issues, and assess risk impact.</p>
